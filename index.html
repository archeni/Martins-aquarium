Types of Fresh Water Fish

Alligator gar
  • Atrctosteus spatula
  • southwestern Ohio and southern Illinois to the Mississippi River drainage basin
  • Alligator gar feed on blue crabs, turtles, waterfowl or other birds and small mammals
  • 200cm
Humpback chub
  • Gila cypha
  • Little Colorado River
  • algae, plant debris, ﬁsh remains, Mormon crickets and other terrestrial insects
  • 25cm
Lake sturgeon
  • Acipenser fulvescens
  • Mississippi River, Hudson Bay and the Great Lakes
  • Lake sturgeon are omnivorous and eat insect larvae, crayﬁsh, snails, clams and leeches
  • 140kg
Channel catfish
  • Ictalurus punctatus
  • southern Canada into northern Mexico
  • small ﬁsh, crustaceans (crayﬁsh), clams and snails, aquatic insects and small mammals
  • 57cm
Fish and Tank Care
  • An aquarium is a delicately balanced ecosystem that relies on “good” bacteria to keep fish healthy. When everything is working right, helpful bacteria grow in the aquarium’s gravel and filter. There, they help break down the ammonia and nitrite found in fish waste—which, if left unchecked, would be toxic to your fish.
  • Be sure to check the water temperature in your tank every day.
  • Replace 10% of the tank with clean water, test the water's pH, ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, hardness, alkalinity and chlorine, and clean away any algae weekly
